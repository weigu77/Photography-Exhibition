Prologue: The Gate of Time and Space

This introductory chapter presents the Forbidden City in Beijing, the National Centre for the Performing Arts in China, the Shesheng Tower in Shanxi, and the Tower Bridge in London as the prologue to an exploratory journey. From the grand ancient palaces to modern skyscrapers, the Forbidden City showcases the long history and contemporary style of Chinese architecture; the ancient architectural complex in Shanxi narrates a story of a thousand years; while London's Tower Bridge stands as a steel poem crossing the River Thames. These four iconic structures not only each bear rich cultural and historical significance but also foreshadow the vast domains to be explored in the following four chapters, inviting you to appreciate the brilliant chapters of human civilization.

Title: The Forbidden City, Beijing

Resource Type: Ancient architectural ensemble

Date: Ming and Qing dynasties

Photographer: Bingcheng Lu

Place of origin: China

Descripition:The Palace Museum, located in Beijing, China, served as the royal palace of the Ming and Qing dynasties and was formerly known as the Forbidden City. It is situated at the center of Beijing's central axis. The Palace Museum is one of the largest and most well-preserved ancient wooden architectural complexes in the world

Title: National Centre for the Performing Arts of China

Resource Type: Public buildings

Date: Completed in 2007

Photographer: Yucen Gao

Place of origin: China

Descripition:The National Centre for the Performing Arts in China, one of the new 'Sixteen Scenic Spots of Beijing,' is a landmark building designed by the French architect Paul Andreu. The exterior of the National Centre for the Performing Arts is in a semi-ellipsoidal shape and is equipped with facilities such as an opera house, concert hall, drama theater, art exhibition halls, restaurants, and audio-visual shops.

Title: Relic Pagoda

Resource Type: Buddhist architecture

Date: Built in 1751

Photographer: Yucen Gao

Place of origin: China

Descripition:The majestic physique and exquisite decorations are highly praised by viewers, representing one of the 'Eight Scenic Views Outside Jin Temple' known as the 'Pagoda Shrouded in Clouds'.

Title: Tower Bridge, London

Resource Type: Upper suspension bridge

Date: It was founded in 1886

Photographer: Barcex

Place of origin: London

Descripition:Tower Bridge is a bascule and suspension bridge located in London, England, spanning the River Thames. It derives its name from its proximity to the Tower of London. It is the first bridge from the mouth of the Thames, among a total of fifteen bridges built over the river, and serves as a symbol of London.
